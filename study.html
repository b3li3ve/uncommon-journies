Equipping: Bible Studies and Helpful Resources

“And [Jesus] said to him, “ ‘You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your mind.’ “This is the great and foremost commandment. “The second is like it, ‘You shall love your neighbor as yourself.’ (Matthew 22:38-39. NASB 95).

At Jamie Hulker: Here for You, we’re all about helping you embrace your God-given design and gifts for His glory and the good of others. We believe it all starts with quality time with God—digging into the Bible to discover who He is and how He guides our lives. We also think great resources can enhance your study and deepen your understanding.

With that in mind, we’ve gathered some of our favorite resources and study tools just for you. We pray these will help you know God better, grow your understanding of His amazing love for you, and inspire you to worship Him more deeply.

Our Bible Studies:

At Jamie Hulker: Here for You, our Bible studies are designed to help followers of Jesus, or those seeking to know more about Him, grow in their understanding of God and His love and grace for humanity. We offer two types of studies:

  • Expository Studies: Designed to study an entire book chapter-by-chapter, verse-by-verse.
  • Topical Studies: Designed to discover what the Bible teaches about a particular idea throughout the Old Testament and New Testament.

Our Studies are crafted to be

  • Feasible: We recognize everyone is busy, so our studies are designed to fit your busy life.
  • Fulfilling: Our studies are informative and will guide you to a deeper understanding of God and how to follow Him.
  • Fun: We ensure our studies are interesting and provide a fun community!

Current Studies Led by Jamie

Esteemed: God and His View of Women

Imagine a Bible study that brings the stories of women in Scripture to life with the same depth and reverence often reserved for other biblical figures. Now, you don’t have to imagine—it’s here. Together, we’ll explore thirty-three accounts of women from the Old and New Testaments, along with three inspiring stories from history. These women, esteemed by God, played crucial roles in His divine plan, standing as heroes and role models for all of us. From national leaders and deliverers to prophetesses, disciples, church planters, missionaries, mothers, and more, their stories offer a biblically and historically grounded view of God’s heart for women.

But this study goes beyond simply recounting others’ stories—it invites you to step into your own God-given purpose. As you uncover fresh insights into how real women, facing real challenges, followed God and served others, you’ll be inspired to follow their example—for His glory and others’ good.

Helpful Resources

The Bible

There are a variety of good Bible translations available. Here are the distinctions and our recommendations for each:

  • A Literal Translation: Stays as close as possible to the original Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek texts, preserving the exact wording and structure (Recommended: New American Standard).
  • A Dynamic translation: Focuses on conveying the meaning of the original text in a way that's easier to read and understand, even if it moves away from the exact wording (Recommended: New Living Translation or New International Version).
  • A Paraphrase:  Rewords the original text to express ideas and concepts using modern language and ideas (Recommended: The Message).
  • The Amplified Version: Combines literal and dynamic approaches, offering expanded translations of keywords for a better understanding of the meaning (Highly recommended, especially if you can’t access other resources).

Valuable Bible Study Tools:

Commentaries, Bible Dictionaries, Lexicons and Concordances

  • Commentaries: A book or series that provides verse-by-verse or passage-by-passage explanations and interpretations of the Bible. Commentaries offer historical, cultural, and theological insights from scholars. (Recommended for beginning Bible students: the New American Commentary, the NIV Application Commentary Series, and the Bible Knowledge Commentary. Recommended for more advanced study: The New International Commentary on the Old Testament (NICOT) and New Testament (NICNT)).
  • Bible Dictionaries: A reference book that provides definitions and explanations of biblical terms, names, places, and concepts. It’s useful for understanding the background and meaning of specific words or topics. (Recommended: The Lexham Bible Dictionary and The New Unger's Bible Dictionary)
  • Lexicons: A dictionary specifically for the original languages of the Bible—Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek—offering definitions, word usage, and grammatical information. It’s helpful for deep word studies. (Recommended: Vines Expository Dictionary of Old and New Testament Words, and The Theological Wordbook of the Old Testament).
  • Concordance: An alphabetical index of words found in the Bible, showing where each word occurs and often providing the original Greek or Hebrew term. It's helpful for locating verses and studying word usage. (Recommended: Strong’s Exhaustive Concordance of the Bible)
